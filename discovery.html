The state of Paraná

Paraná is a state in southern Brazil that’s home to Iguaçu Falls, with hundreds of cascades straddling the border with Argentina. Surrounding the falls is Iguaçu National Park, a subtropical rainforest with diverse wildlife, while north lies the massive Itaipu Dam. Hundreds of kilometers east, close to the Atlantic beaches of Guaratuba and the large port of Paranaguá, is the leafy state capital, Curitiba. Iguaçu Falls are one of the world's largest waterfalls. Stretching for 2.7km, and straddling the border with Argentina, the falls comprise hundreds of cascades, including the 80m-tall Devil’s Throat.

Oscar Niemayer Museum

The Oscar Niemeyer Museum, a state patrimony, located in Curitiba, houses important references of national and international artistic production, with more than 14 thousand works in the areas of visual arts, architecture and design. It is considered the largest art museum in Latin America, with a space of about 35 thousand square meters of built area and more than 17 thousand square meters of area for exhibitions. The Tower, also popularly known as “Eye”, completes the complex with its four floors of exhibition space.

Curitiba Botanic Garden

One of the most visited tourist spots in Curitiba, opened in 1991. Created in the image of French gardens, it extends its flower carpet to visitors right at the entrance. The greenhouse, in a metallic structure, houses botanical species that are national references, in addition to a water source. GARDEN OF SENSATIONS It is on a 200-meter trail in the Botanical Garden of Curitiba, in which the visitor walks blindfolded, knowing with his senses the remaining plants and some natural landscapes. In Jardim das Sensações you can see the plants with your hands, feel the ground with your feet, hear the voice of the wind and the waterfall, breathe in the perfume of the plants and the smell of the earth. It has a seed, a branch, a bell that rings and announces the beauty of life.

Ucrainian Memorial

O Memorial da Imigração Ucraniana, localizado dentro do Parque Tingüi, é um tributo à contribuição desse povo à cultura do Estado. Oito famílias de ucranianos chegaram ao Paraná em 1891, fixando-se na Colônia Santa Bárbara, entre Palmeira e Ponta Grossa,mas foi só a partir de 1895 que as grandes levas de imigrantes fixaram-se nos arredores de Curitiba e, mais tarde, em Prudentópolis e Marechal Mallet. Estima-se que até 1914 cerca de 45 mil imigrantes ucranianos tenham chegado ao Paraná. O monumento principal do Memorial é a réplica da igreja ucraniana de São Miguel, na Serra do Tigre (município de Mallet), uma das mais antigas do país.

Federal University of Paraná

The Federal University of Paraná (UFPR) is the oldest teaching institution with the concept of a university in Brazil, founded on December 19, 1912, initially with the name of Universidade do Paraná. UFPR appears in several rankings as one of the best higher education institutions in Brazil, one of the few to be mentioned in international rankings as well.
